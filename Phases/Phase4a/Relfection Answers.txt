Reflection Questions:

1. Why do we convert continuous values into categories?
Continuous values (such as total_spend) are converted into categories (Gold, Silver, Bronze) to make the data easier to understand, analyze, and use for business decisions.

2. What is the difference between business segmentation and technical bucketing?
Business segmentation uses business-defined rules, whereas technical bucketing groups data into numerical ranges for analysis and machine learning.

3. When would fixed thresholds fail?
Fixed thresholds may fail when the data distribution changes over time.

4. How does quantile-based segmentation differ from fixed rules?
Fixed rules use predefined thresholds, whereas quantile-based segmentation determines thresholds from the data to create balanced groups.
                                                       
5. Which method would you use in a real-world project?
I would use quantile-based segmentation for analytics and customer insights because it adapts to changing data and creates balanced customer groups.
